Perception of Safety in an Urban Environment

- The study identified latent and underlying factors that can be related to the “perception of safety”.
- An intent to analyze and understand these characteristics of neighborhoods that do show an understandable impact on this perception of safety, which are based on data-driven features such as socioeconomic, demographic, and physical characteristics were also observed and studied.
- Much of the analysis used has been the Ordinary Least Square (OLS) regression technique within Python.
- A LASSO (Least Absolute Shrinkage and Selection Operator) method of regression has been applied for the absolute features selection from different datasets


Skills Used: Multivariate Regression, PCA, Lasso, Cross Validation, ArcGIS

Analyzed Spatial-temporal patterns of 311 service request activity in NYC.

- The specific challenge was to analyze the incoming service requests from 2015 –approximately two million in total – and attempt to gain insight on the socioeconomic conditions within New York City.
- using Python and geo pandas, The Spatial Analysis gave me the idea where the complaints were made and therefore I visualized using ArcGIS what kind of similar complaints has the corresponding neighborhood.
- In the same way, the temporal analysis yelled results of same complaints at the same time. a time span of one week has been split into 56 bins with one bin corresponding to 3 hour period cell, the formula for which being: 3 hour /times/ 4 summing up to 24 hours /times/ 7 days.
- Computational approach for the research included Python with pandas package and K-means clustering technique.
